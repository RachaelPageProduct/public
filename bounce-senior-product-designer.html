How I'd tackle Bounce

The way I'd work in this role.

Not starting from blank. Starting from understanding what's working and what isn't — then iterating fast.

01

Get inside the product and the data fast

Before designing anything, I'd spend the first week using Bounce as a traveller, reviewing analytics and prior research, and talking to anyone on the team who knows where the real pain points are. I don't assume I know the problem before I've seen the evidence.

02

Design in the open — no big reveals

I work iteratively and share early. Messy Figma frames beat polished decks that surprise engineers two weeks later. I'd rather show a low-fi concept in a Slack thread and get a fast "no" than build the wrong thing to perfection.

03

Ship first, refine second

You're a fast-moving, scrappy team. I operate the same way. Viable beats perfect — I know when something is good enough to ship and when it genuinely needs more work. The Ticklists MVP is a live example: functional, in production, deliberately not over-polished until the concept is validated.

04

Maintain the design system as infrastructure, not overhead

Design systems only stay consistent if someone treats them as a living product. I'd work with whatever exists at Bounce, extend it pragmatically, and flag inconsistencies before they compound — without slowing the team down to polish tokens nobody will see.
