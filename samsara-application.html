How I'd work at Samsara

The way I'd approach this role.

Not starting from blank. Starting from understanding Samsara's users, the existing product, and where AI and agentic design can create the most impact — fast.

01

Get inside the product and the users immediately

Before designing anything, I'd spend the first week using Samsara's platform as a fleet operator would, reviewing existing research and analytics, and talking to anyone on the team who knows where the real friction is. I don't assume I know the problem before I've seen the evidence — especially in a domain as operationally specific as this.

02

Design AI workflows that solve real operational problems

Agentic UX is genuinely new territory — most teams are still working out what it should feel like. My advantage is that I use AI tools daily, so I understand the mental model users need: when to trust automation, when to stay in control, and how to make handoffs between human and AI feel natural rather than alarming. That instinct comes from use, not theory.

03

Design in the open — no big reveals

I work iteratively and share early. Messy Figma frames beat polished decks that surprise engineers two weeks later. I'd rather show a low-fi concept in a Slack thread and get a fast "no" than build the wrong thing to perfection. The rebrand work in particular benefits from lots of early, low-stakes review cycles.

04

Treat the design system as infrastructure, not a deliverable

A rebrand-supporting design system only works if it's maintained as a living product. I'd work with whatever exists at Samsara, extend it pragmatically to support the new direction, and flag inconsistencies before they compound — without slowing the team down to polish tokens that nobody ships with yet.
