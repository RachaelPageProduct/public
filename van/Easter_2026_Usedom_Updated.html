Where You'll Sleep

Your Two Stellplätze

ℹ️ Do you need to book? Neither place requires formal booking — both are pay-on-arrival. However, Karnin 19 only has 3 pitches and Uli (the owner) is personally on site. It's worth a quick WhatsApp or call before you leave just to flag you're coming: Park4Night has his contact. Karlshagen harbour stellplatz is a municipal carpark — always space, just park and pay the machine.

Karnin 19 — Piratencafé Stellplatz

📍 Karnin 19, 17406 Usedom
Thursday night only · Peenestrom water views

~€15per night
🌊Right on the Peenestrom estuary. Views of the historic Karnin railway bridge. Flat, quiet, wild feeling.
🍔Ibbis Hafencafé (Piratencafé) is literally next door — homemade burgers, cakes, hammocks, dog-friendly. Uli the owner is warm and helpful.
🚿Toilets and showers on-site (coin, €1–1.50). Van has its own shower so these are just a backup.
🔌Electricity available at extra cost. Not needed if van is self-sufficient.
🐕Dogs very welcome. Space to walk along the estuary banks.
📞Open all year. Contact via Park4Night (place 507566) to give Uli a heads-up.
✅ Open all year No booking required

Jachthafen Karlshagen — Peenestraße 60

📍 Peenestraße 60, 17449 Karlshagen
Fri / Sat / Sun nights · Peenestrom + harbour views

€12per night
🌊Municipal stellplatz at the yacht harbour on the Peenestrom. Quiet, low-key, proper van-life feel. Pine trees and water nearby.
🚿WC and showers on-site (clean, coin-operated). Not needed but handy for a top-up.
🏖️The Baltic beach is ~1.5km walk/cycle — Orla's morning run territory.
🚲Flat cycle path right outside connects you to Trassenheide and Zinnowitz (surf school ~7km).
💳Pay at machine — coins or app. No check-in, no warden. Just park up.
🅿️Suitable for larger vans. Flat, hardstanding near the harbour.
✅ Open all year No booking needed — just arrive

Activity — Solo

Your Windsurfing Plan

Sail-Away Usedom · Beach 8Q · Zinnowitz

📍 Strandzugang 8Q, 17454 Zinnowitz · 0171 21 22 676 · [email protected]

~€30–35solo · 1–2h taster
🏄
Just a taster session — one afternoon, one day. Rather than committing to the full 10-hour course, you'll do 1–2 hours on the water to get a feel for windsurfing on the Baltic. If you love it, you can come back for the full course another time in better weather.
📋
Sail-Away offer single hours and taster sessions as well as full courses. All equipment included — board, sail, wetsuit, life jacket. Contact them to book a single session slot: 0171 21 22 676 or [email protected].
🌬️
If wind is poor on the day, they'll advise rescheduling. April Baltic winds are usually decent but can be variable — worth having a flexible afternoon for this.

🚲 Getting there — cycling with the doggy cart

🚲
Cycle from Karlshagen to Zinnowitz: ~7km on the flat Ostseeküstenradweg coastal path — about 25–30 minutes at a relaxed pace. Orla rides in the doggy cart.
🔒
Lock the doggy cart at the bike racks at beach 8Q — they're right outside Sail-Away and clearly visible from the Surfbar terrace where Brunow can sit and keep an eye on it.
🎨
Brunow + Orla hang out at the beach while you're on the water — he can sit at Surfbar 8Q (if open), walk along the strand, or find a café on the promenade.
🚲
Cycle back together afterwards — 7km home, maybe stopping for a post-surf drink in Zinnowitz first.
✅ One day, one activity. The surf session is the activity for that afternoon. Cycle there, surf 1–2h, cycle back. That's a full and satisfying day without overdoing it.

Activity — Solo

Horse Riding

Solo ride — just you. Brunow is happy to stay at the stellplatz with Orla while you go. This halves the cost and means no pressure on him.

Friesenhof Reit- & Freizeithotel

📍 Bahnhofstraße 48, 17449 Trassenheide · +49 38371 2610 · [email protected] · friesenhof-usedom.de

~€40
1 hour guided ride · solo
⭐Rated 8.0/10 on Booking.com from 416 verified reviews. Guests specifically praise the well-maintained horses and stables and friendly, attentive riding staff. A recent reviewer described staff as "so nice, friendly and sweet" — a well-run, caring stable.
🐴Around 20 horses — Friesians, warmbloods and riding ponies. As an experienced rider, tell them your level upfront and ask for a horse that suits a confident rider who wants a real outing, not a led walk. Helmets and tack provided.
🌲Riding through the coastal pine forest of Trassenheide and onto the beach. Beach riding in April is in the permitted window at Trassenheide — the official beach riding season runs to around April here. Confirm when you book.
📅Book ahead — call +49 38371 2610 or email [email protected]. Ask for a Sunday morning guided trail or beach ride, 1 experienced rider, and mention you've been riding since childhood.
🚲Only 2km from your Karlshagen stellplatz — a very easy 5-minute cycle. Brunow and Orla come too; lock the doggy cart at the stables while you ride.
📅 Book in advance

6 Apr
South Usedom Explorer · Then Home both
🗺️🏡
08:30
Final harbour walk with Orla. Pack up, pay the machine, say goodbye to Karlshagen. Coffee from the van before you go.
09:30
Head south along the coastal road — not the fast exit road. You're doing a slow loop through the south of the island on the way out. No rush, no must-sees, just tasting the other half of Usedom.
~09:50
① Streckelsberg, Koserow — pull over and stretch your legs. This is the third highest point on Usedom — a 58m coastal cliff dune formed in the last ice age, right above the sea. Short walk to the top = panoramic views over the Baltic on one side and the Achterwasser lagoon on the other. Orla off-leash in the dunes. Takes 20–30 minutes. 📍 Between Zempin and Koserow, signed from the road.
~10:30
② Koserow Salzhütten — walk down to the beach at Koserow and have a look at the historic wooden salt huts, listed buildings from around 1900. One houses a tiny fishing museum, one is a smoked fish restaurant (evening only, but the fish stall outside sells Fischbrötchen from the morning). Charming, very un-touristy in April. 15 minutes. 📍 Koserow beach promenade, near the new pier.
~11:00
③ Ückeritz & the Stagnieß Harbour — cross to the Achterwasser side of the island (5 min drive from Koserow, no ferry). Ückeritz is one of the amber beaches — worth a walk along the shore. The tiny natural harbour at Stagnieß is one of the most peaceful spots on the whole island: reeds, fishing boats, utter silence. Orla can paddle. This is the sort of place you'd come back for a longer stay. 📍 Follow signs for Stagnieß from Ückeritz — tiny road, worth it. ~20 minutes.
~11:30
④ Wasserschloss Mellenthin — continue south (10 min drive from Ückeritz). This 1575 Renaissance moated castle is surrounded by a wide moat and ancient oaks. Dogs welcome in the grounds. Own brewery, coffee roastery and café — the perfect lunch stop. Walk the moat path, watch for storks on the rooftops. Orla loves the grounds. 📍 Schlossallee 5, 17429 Mellenthin · Café opens 12:00 — time it right for a waffle and coffee before leaving · wasserschloss-mellenthin.de
~13:00
Head home. Mellenthin is already in the south of the island — exit directly south via the Zecherin bridge → B110 → A20 → B96 → Falkenberg. About 2h from here.
~15:00
Home. Still well within mid-afternoon. ✓ Unpacked, cups of tea, Orla asleep, Brunow sketching the storks from memory.

Eat & Drink

Cafés & Restaurants

📍 Karnin (Thursday evening)

Ibbis Hafencafé / Piratencafé ⭐⭐

Karnin 19 · right next to your stellplatz

Uli's café attached to the stellplatz. Homemade burgers, the best potato salad on the island, cakes, coffee, hammock seating with estuary views. Reviewers consistently call it a hidden gem. Warm, personal, unhurried.

🐕 Dog-friendly ☕ Café
📍 Karlshagen (your base, Fri–Sun)

Restaurant Waldhäuschen ⭐⭐⭐

Karlshagen town · no closing day

The best restaurant in Karlshagen by consistent reviews. Small menu, everything made with love, owner is warm and attentive. Dogs explicitly welcome. Kitchen closes 8pm — arrive by 7:30. Worth booking for one evening.

🐕 Dog-friendly 🍽️ Dinner

Veermaster ⭐⭐

Am Hafen 2, Karlshagen · open from 11:30 daily

Classic Baltic seafood at the harbour. Outdoor terrace directly on the Peenestrom — great spot for a celebratory lunch or lazy Sunday dinner. Good fish, relaxed atmosphere.

🐕 Terrace OK 🐟 Seafood

Wolfsteller Bakery

Strandpromenade 8 · Mon–Sun 07:00–18:00

Morning coffee and pastries — open from 07:00 daily. Good stop on the way out on the bikes, or a first morning coffee on Friday after arriving from Karnin.

☕ Café / Bakery

Fischimbiss Pier Nr.1

Hafenpromenade · 11:00–17:00 daily

No-frills harbour fish shack. Smoked flounder, herring rolls, cheap and fresh. Ideal quick harbour lunch for Brunow and Orla on any day.

📍 Zinnowitz area (near the surf school)

Surfbar 8Q ⭐⭐

Strandzugang 8Q · literally next to Sail-Away

Beachside bar right at the surf school. Where you celebrate after your session with Brunow and Orla. Officially May–Sept — check if open for Easter. If so, this is the obvious post-surf drink stop before cycling home.

🌊 Beachside

Onkel Ben's Breakfast & Lunch ⭐⭐

Neue Strandstraße 37, Zinnowitz · open until 3pm

Highly praised for brunch and light lunches. Cosy inner courtyard patio. Great scrambled eggs, fair prices. Good stop in Zinnowitz before or after the Saturday surf session.

☕ Brunch

Backbord Café & Bäckerei

Neue Strandstraße 22, Zinnowitz · 07:00–18:00 daily

Early-opening bakery with good coffee and fresh bread. Good stop on the way into Zinnowitz on the bike before a surf session or a browse of the promenade.

☕ Bakery

Kombüse

Hafenstraße 3, Zinnowitz · Wed–Sat 11:30–21:00

Proper sit-down restaurant near the Zinnowitz harbour. Good option for a post-surf lunch on Saturday after your windsurf session before cycling back. ⚠️ No dogs inside — Brunow would need to sit outside or skip this one.

🐟 Lunch/Dinner

Café Central

Neue Strandstraße 24, Zinnowitz · 10:00–22:00

All-day reliable option on the promenade. Good for Brunow to have a coffee while waiting, or a post-surf snack with a sea view.
