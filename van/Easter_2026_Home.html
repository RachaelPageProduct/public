The Plan at a Glance

Four Days, Four Moods

No van this time, but four very different days — one on bikes along a century-old railway line, one deep in a Brandenburg forest with a glacial lake, one slow Easter Sunday at home, and one evening splurge on fajitas with a river view. All very Orla-friendly. All under €80.

🚲 Friday — Oderbruchbahn

Drive 25min to Müncheberg. Cycle the old trainline through apple orchards, moraine hills and lake edges to Schloss Steinhöfel park. Turn around and ride back. ~28km total, mostly asphalted, zero hills worth mentioning.

🚲 Bike day 🐕 Orla in doggy cart 28km

🏺 Saturday — Karlshorst Flohmarkt + Fajitas

Drive 45min to Berlin-Karlshorst for the Easter season-opening antique flea market on the old trotting racecourse — 500 dealers, 25,000m², free entry. Back home by 3pm, then out again for dinner at Tip Steakhouse, Fürstenwalde.

🐕 Dogs welcome on market grounds 🎪 Free entry

🌲 Sunday — Scharmützelsee

Drive 30min to Bad Saarow. Walk the Rauener Berge certified trail through deep mixed forest, past 1.45-billion-year-old glacial boulders and up a 45m lookout tower. Finish on the lake promenade. 15.5km of proper nature.

🐕 Orla off-leash in forest 🌲 Free entry 15km walk

🐴 Monday — Local Ride or Slow Day

Option A: book a morning hack at one of the 5 nearby stables listed below — all within 35km, all open Mondays. Option B: no car, local tracks around Falkenberg, Orla off-leash, Brunow sketches, home cooking. Either way, a good last day.

🐕 Orla in the fields 🏡 No driving required

Day by Day

The Itinerary

Fri
3 Apr
Oderbruchbahn Cycle Day 🚲 both
🚲🐕
Morning
Slow start at home. Pack the doggy cart, fill the water bottles, throw together a picnic. Leave around 9:30–10:00 — no need to rush.
~10:00
Drive to Müncheberg (~25km, 25 minutes from Falkenberg). Park near the Bahnhof. Pick up the Oderbruchbahn-Radweg — signed with the green railway logo on a yellow background. Orla goes in the doggy cart.
~10:30
Ride south towards Steinhöfel (~14km, gently rolling). The route follows the bed of the old Oderbruchbahn narrow-gauge railway (1912–1970s). Almost nobody around in April. Apple trees line the old track — planted from pips thrown from carriage windows over a century of journeys. Lakes appear through the trees. Dorfkirche after Dorfkirche. Storks on almost every rooftop.Surface: mostly asphalted. A few short gravel sections. Doggy cart handles it fine.
~11:30
Stop at Schloss Steinhöfel park. One of the earliest English landscape gardens in all of Brandenburg, designed in the 1780s. Free to walk — wide gravel paths through ancient trees, a pond, Chinese pavilion, and views back to the Schloss. Orla can roam freely in April — no summer rules yet. Perfect picnic spot. Brunow will want to sketch.
~12:30
Coffee stop — Café am Lindenplatz, Müncheberg (on the ride back, 500m off route into town). Central café behind the Stadtpfarrkirche, outdoor seating under linden trees, coffee and cake. Dog-friendly. Cheaper and calmer than anything in Steinhöfel.Or: Landmarkt farm shop in Steinhöfel on the outward leg — hot drinks, simple snacks, fresh local produce.
~13:30
Ride back to Müncheberg (~14km). Same track, different light. Stop at the Milchtankstelle near Buchholz if you pass it — a literal fresh-milk vending machine from local cows. Cold, rich, genuinely delicious.
~14:30
Back to the car. Drive home. ~25 min. Feet up, Orla asleep, a glass of something cold.
Evening
Home cooking. A proper keto-vegan dinner. Early-ish night — tomorrow is the Steakhouse, so a good home meal tonight means you arrive hungry on Saturday.

4 Apr
Easter🐣
Karlshorst Antik-Flohmarkt + Steakhouse 🎪🥩
🏺🥩
~09:00
Drive to Berlin-Karlshorst (~55km, ~45 min). Antikflohmarkt Trabrennbahn Karlshorst — Easter Special & Season Opening. The Easter weekend event is the biggest of the year: 300–500 professional dealers across 25,000m² of the former trotting racecourse, plus a 1,000m² covered hall. Antique furniture, Bauhaus, Midcentury, DDR, vintage clothing, ceramics, art, collectables. Free entry. Opens 09:00. 📍 Treskowallee 159, 10318 Berlin-Karlshorst · Street parking free nearby · Dogs welcome on the grounds
Morning
Brunow in his element. Orla on a lead between the stalls. This market attracts dealers from all over Germany — the Easter opening weekend always has exceptional stock. On Sunday there's also an Easter egg hunt and the traditional Osterfeuer bonfire in the evening — but Saturday is prime browsing time before it gets busy.
~13:00
Street food on site. The market has food stalls throughout — a good lunch stop before the drive back without spending extra.
~14:30
Drive home (~45 min). Unload whatever Brunow found. Rest, change, let Orla have a run in the garden.
~18:00
Drive to Fürstenwalde (~30km, ~30 min). Tip Steakhouse for 19:00. Book ahead — Easter weekend, it will be busy. The fajita sizzle plate, river view. Keto approach: double the protein, skip the tortillas, extra guacamole. Search "Tip Steakhouse Fürstenwalde" on Google Maps to confirm address and book.
~21:00
Drive home. Market day and a good dinner — that's a proper Easter Saturday.
Sun
5 Apr
Rauener Berge & Scharmützelsee 🌲 both
🌲🗻
09:30
Drive to Bad Saarow (~30km, 30 minutes from Falkenberg via B246/L412). Park free at the Bahnhof Bad Saarow-Pieskow (52.2708°N, 14.0710°E) or on Seestraße. Orla on the lead until you're in the forest.
~10:00
Start the Rauener Berge Rundweg — a certified "Traumtour" (German Hiking Association). 15.5km, ~4–4.5h at a relaxed pace with Orla. Route: Bahnhof → Scharmützelsee promenade north → into the Rauener Berge forest → Markgrafensteine → Aussichtsturm → Petersdorfer See → loop back to Bahnhof.
~11:00
The Markgrafensteine (52.3196°N, 14.0353°E). Two enormous glacial granite boulders — the largest erratic boulders in all of northern Germany, 1.45 billion years old, carried here from Scandinavia by ice sheets. Something properly humbling about leaning against a rock that existed before complex life on Earth. Orla will try to pee on them. This is acceptable.
~11:30
Aussichtsturm der Rauener Berge — €1 per person. 45m wooden observation tower with views over the Scharmützelsee and on a clear April day, the Berlin TV tower to the west. Worth every cent.
~13:00
Petersdorfer See. A Gaststätte right on the smaller lake before the loop back — dog-friendly terrace, coffee, simple food. Orla can paddle at the edge.
~14:30
Scharmützelsee promenade. Theodor Fontane called this the "Märkisches Meer" — at 1,210 hectares, you understand why. Walk the lakeside path as far as it feels right. Dedicated Hundestrand (dog beach) at the Wasserwanderrastplatz — Orla can swim. Cold April lake, she'll love it.
~16:00
Drive home (30 min). Long hot shower. Brunow sketches the boulders from memory. Orla dries off and collapses.
Evening
Home cooking. You've done 15km on foot — a good satisfying keto-vegan dinner and an early night.

Monday Option — Horse Riding

5 Nearest Stables

All within 35km. Call ahead to book — mention you're an experienced rider since childhood and want a trail hack or hack in the countryside, not a beginner lesson.

1
Feldreiterhof Molkenberg ⭐⭐⭐
📍 Molkenberg 12, 15517 Fürstenwalde · ~30km · +49 163 6850593

Rated 4.9★ from 32 reviews — the highest rated in the area. Family-run boarding stable with 24/7 pasture access, known for proper animal welfare. Reviewers specifically praise the horses being "genuinely allowed to be horses." Also has a hand-reared bison called Hannibal on site. Combine with the Steakhouse drive easily.

2
Flower Horse Ranch, Falkenberg ⭐⭐⭐
📍 Dorfstraße 11/1, 16259 Falkenberg · ~35km north · +49 162 4878433

4.8★, 38 reviews. A Western-style ranch with free-roaming horses — reviewers describe "surprising and wonderful encounters" with horses that come to you. One reviewer specifically noted it's campervan-friendly too. "A very compassionate riding instructor." Different in feel to a traditional Reiterhof — more relaxed and open.

3
Camargue-Pferde-Hof, Altlandsberg ⭐⭐
📍 Am Park 2, 15345 Altlandsberg · ~30km west · +49 3341 49877280

4.5★, 139 reviews. Specialises in Camargue horses — distinctive white horses known for their good temperament and natural gaits. Set within a park with good forest riding territory. Brunow and Orla can walk the park while you ride. Regular events and trail rides offered.

4
Galgenberghof, Müncheberg ⭐⭐
📍 Eberswalder Str. 158a, 15374 Müncheberg · ~25km north · +49 174 3928029

4.5★, 181 reviews. Conveniently located right by the Oderbruchbahn bike route start — if you do the bike day on Friday and want to return to the same area on Monday on horseback, this works perfectly. Hack into the Märkische Schweiz countryside from here. Forest paths directly accessible.

5
Reiterhof Waldkante, Altlandsberg ⭐⭐
📍 Waldkante 3, 15345 Altlandsberg · ~30km west · +49 173 4471226

4.4★, 67 reviews. Forest-edge stable — the name says it all. Direct access to riding trails through woodland. Well-cared-for horses, knowledgeable instructors noted by reviewers. Note: closed Sundays, so Monday is a good fit. Call ahead to confirm Bank Holiday availability.

When calling: Say you've been riding since childhood and want a trail ride or hack in the countryside — not a lesson and not a led walk. Ask specifically what the terrain is like for experienced adult riders. Most of these stables will have something suitable, but you need to ask directly.

Eat & Drink

The Two Café Stops

One meal out, and two café stops on the bike route. That's the budget. Both are on Friday's route so you don't have to drive anywhere extra.

☕ On the Bike Route — Friday

Café am Lindenplatz ⭐

Behind the Stadtpfarrkirche · Müncheberg · ~500m off route

Central Müncheberg café with outdoor seating under the shade of linden trees. Coffee, homemade cake, light lunches. The kind of place that's been there forever and won't fuss about a dog or a muddy doggy cart out front. Stop on the ride back when you've earned it.

🐕 Dog-friendly outdoors 🚲 On the route

Landmarkt Steinhöfel

Steinhöfel · on the route at ~10km mark

A farm shop with hot drinks and simple snacks — not a café exactly, but a good early stop on the outward leg before Schloss Steinhöfel. Fresh local produce, warm drinks. The kind of stop that makes a bike ride feel like an actual outing rather than just exercise.

🐕 Outdoor 🚲 Right on route
🥩 Saturday Evening — Fürstenwalde

Tip Steakhouse Fürstenwalde ⭐⭐

Fürstenwalde/Spree · ~30min drive · book ahead for Easter Sunday

Your first visit — and the whole reason for the Sunday evening. The fajita sizzle plate with a river view over the Spree is the thing to order. Keto-friendly approach: load up on the protein, skip the tortillas, extra guacamole and sour cream on the side. American diner feel with a Spree terrace. Reviewers consistently say the staff are friendly and it's a genuinely fun atmosphere.

🌊 River view 🥩 Keto-friendly options
On chai tea: This area is not known for it — but it's worth asking at the Tip Steakhouse (American-style menus sometimes have it), and the Café am Lindenplatz in Müncheberg may have it as a specialty tea. If you're making chai at home, bring a good blend — a proper masala chai made in the van before the Friday ride beats most café versions anyway.
