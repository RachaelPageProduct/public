Where You'll Sleep

Friday Night Stellplatz

Friedrich-Engels-Damm 96/98, Bad Saarow

📍 15526 Bad Saarow · Park4Night #193137 · ~30km from Falkenberg (GPS: 52.3822°N 14.2369°E)

Freetolerated overnight
🌊Water view from the van. Large wooded car park 1 minute's walk from the Scharmützelsee shore. Through the trees to the right you see the lake. Wake up, open the van doors, brew your tea — lake immediately visible. It's exactly what you want.
🏊5-minute walk to the main beach and dedicated Hundestrand (dog beach) where Orla can swim freely. Big open meadow with two permanent toilet huts on the beach in summer.
🚐Overnight parking tolerated by the Ordnungsamt — confirmed by multiple Park4Night reviewers. Rule: no outdoor chairs, no cooking outside, no signs of camping. Sleep in the van, use your own bathroom, and you'll be left alone. Arrive after 6pm, leave before 10am.
🐕Dogs welcome. Leash in the car park, off-leash on the Hundestrand.
📍GPS: 52.2544°N, 14.0472°E · On Friedrich-Engels-Damm, Bad Saarow. Enter from the north end off L412.
Free · no facilities No outdoor furniture 🐕 Dogs welcome
Alternative if the car park is full: Parkplatz Silberstraße / Ecke Heidestraße — a second free car park in Bad Saarow, 10 min walk to the lake. Less idyllic but workable.

Sat
Lake Day · No Driving 🚲🏊 both
🚲🐕💦
Morning
Tea and coffee with a lake view — the whole point of Friday's drive. No alarm. Orla gets a morning run on the Hundestrand. Let the day reveal itself.
~10:00
Get the bikes out. The Scharmützelsee has a well-signed cycle path around much of its perimeter — flat, beautiful, through pine forest with constant lake glimpses. Orla in the doggy cart. Cycle as far as feels good: the south shore via Wendisch Rietz (~15km loop) is the classic route. Stop wherever looks inviting. The lake is 12km long — you can do a short loop of 10km or a longer one of 20–25km. Your call on the day.
~12:30
Freilich am See — lakeside lunch stop, 1km from the stellplatz. Big terrace directly on the water, outside fireplace, children's play area (good for Orla to snoop around). Fish, salads, solid German mains. Their lake perch is the thing to order. 📍 Karl-Marx-Damm 47a, Bad Saarow · Open from 12:00 · +49 33631 594805
Afternoon
Option A — more lake. Cycle onwards around the south shore, or find a quiet spot on the beach and swim. The Scharmützelsee water quality is consistently rated excellent. Orla paddles. Brunow paints. You do whatever you like.
Option B — Rauener Berge walk. Lock the bikes at the Bahnhof Bad Saarow-Pieskow and do part of the certified Rundweg — you don't have to do all 15.5km. Even 6–8km gets you to the Markgrafensteine (the billion-year-old boulders) and back. Orla off-leash in the forest.
~16:30
Back to the van. Swim or shower. The SaarowTherme is 10 minutes' walk if you want a proper spa warm-up — thermal pools, sauna, lake views from the roof terrace. €15–20pp, dogs wait in the van.
Evening — Option A
Van cooking by the lake. Simple, good, free. Watch the last light go off the Scharmützelsee through the windscreen. The best kind of Saturday evening.
Evening — Option B
Das Dorsch — a proper dinner out. Elevated lakeside restaurant on a deck above the water, sunset views, excellent fish and regional dishes. One of the best restaurants on the Scharmützelsee. 4.7★, 1,361 reviews. Closed Mon/Tue — Sat is ideal. 📍 Humboldtstraße 16, 15526 Bad Saarow · Open from 12:00 Sat · +49 33631 2404 · Book ahead

Saturday Night — Pick Your Spot

7 Water-View Stellplatz Options

After the courses finish (~17:00), you drive to wherever you want to wake up Sunday morning. All 7 are within ~45 minutes of the school and ~45 minutes of Falkenberg. All have water. All work for Orla.

Before you go: Always verify current status on Park4Night — wild and tolerated spots can change. Search by GPS or name. Paid spots should be booked ahead for summer weekends.
1
Historisches Fährhaus / Yachthafen — Wendenschloss PAID · best water view
📍 Müggelbergallee 1, Berlin-Wendenschloss · GPS: 52.4271°N, 13.6124°E · ~10 min from school · ~40 min to home · ~€32/night + €3 dog

The only confirmed water-view-from-the-van stellplatz in this area. Directly on the Dahme/Spree, idyllic yacht marina setting in a historic villa district. Wooden terraces with table and chairs, sauna on site, boat hire. Clean facilities, friendly harbour master. VisitBerlin confirmed: "direct view of the water from your camper van." Seasonal April–October — book ahead for summer weekends.

🌊 Guaranteed water view 🐕 Dogs welcome (+€3)

Directly on the Dahme riverbank in a working yacht marina — boats bob past the van all evening. REWE supermarket 50m away. Köpenick old town 3km by foot. VisitBerlin listed. Seasonal April–October. 📍 Schmöckwitz, Berlin-Köpenick · GPS: ~52.3810°N, 13.6670°E · ~25 min from school · ~40 min to home · campsite pricing

On a wooded peninsula between the Krossinsee, Zeuthener See and Seddinsee — three lakes surrounding you on almost all sides. A maritime, village feel with yacht clubs and fish smokehouses nearby in Schmöckwitz. Large site, motorhomes accepted. Own beach and boat dock. One of the most beautiful natural settings of all 7 options. Sunday morning: swim in the lake from the campsite beach.
