Four Lakes on the Way Home "All new, all dog-friendly, all van-accessible"

Orla 🐕 · Large old van · Home via Falkenberg

🚐 Van note applied throughout: All options below are on tarmac roads only — no gravel tracks, no forest paths, no tight turns. Flagged where any uncertainty exists.

Groß Glienicker See

Berlin-Kladow / Potsdam border · ~10 min south from Spandau
↙ Slight detour south before heading home

Barely 10 minutes from where you wake up. This lake was literally divided by the Berlin Wall — the border ran through the centre, marked by buoys, while East Germans were prevented from even seeing the shore. Now a peaceful, clear glacial lake with good water quality, sandy bathing spots, and pine forest on both banks. The old Alexander House on the north-west shore has a remarkable history of its own. Low-key and beautiful.

Parking & Access
📍Verlängerte Uferpromenade, Kladow (east/Berlin shore). Large free car park at the waterfront used by swimmers. Rated 4.2★ on Campercontact.
52.4695°N, 13.1290°E
🛣️Approach via tarmac through residential Kladow — the road is a standard paved residential street all the way to the lake.
🐕Dogs fine along the shore. No formal dog beach but the informal bank areas are relaxed.
💶Free Parking
⚠️The Uferpromenade road narrows toward the waterfront end — fine for normal vehicles, but verify your turning space before committing. Check on arrival.

Tegeler See

Berlin-Tegel / Reinickendorf · ~20 min north from Spandau
↑ Northward detour — backtrack to head home

Berlin's second largest lake, and the quiet north end near Tegelort is genuinely lovely — wooded shore, good visibility in the water, completely different feel from the busy Strandbad end. A nature reserve borders the north side. It's in the wrong direction if you're heading home east, but worth knowing about. The main Strandbad car park is a large, straightforward tarmac lot — no awkward manoeuvring for a big van.

Parking & Access
📍Strandbad Tegeler See car park — large open tarmac, on Greenwichpromenade. Straightforward access, no barriers.
52.5875°N, 13.2783°E
🛣️Access via Greenwichpromenade / Karolinenstraße — standard tarmac roads through Tegel, no issues for a large van.
🐕Dogs welcome along the shore outside the formal Strandbad swimming area.
💶Free Parking  Strandbad entry ~€4pp if using the beach
🏠If you ever want to overnight here: Marina Lanke Wohnmobilstellplatz (Scharfe Lanke 109, GPS 52.5028°N 13.1872°E) — €35/night for up to 7.5m. Dogs welcome. Open May–October.

Liepnitzsee

Wandlitz, Brandenburg · ~35 min north from Spandau
↑ Northern loop — worth it for the colour alone

The one to go to if you want somewhere genuinely spectacular and different. Famous as the only turquoise lake near Berlin — crystal clear, emerald-green water visible 7 metres down, surrounded by pine forest. Nothing like the Müggelsee or Scharmützelsee. A small island in the middle. The campsite is a proper motorhome-grade facility (55+ pitches, paved internal roads, disposal station), which means the access road is solid tarmac designed for large vehicles. The longest detour of the four but the most memorable.

Parking & Access
📍Campingplatz Am Liepnitzsee, Am Liepnitzsee 8, 16348 Wandlitz OT Lanke. Motorhome-grade pitches with electricity. 55+ spaces.
52.7183°N, 13.5003°E
🛣️Approach via A10/A11 motorway then B273 — all tarmac, designed for motorhomes. The campsite road is paved throughout. No forest tracks needed.
🐕Dogs welcome throughout the campsite and on the lake shore.
💶Day visit / parking: ask on arrival (likely €5–10)  Overnight from €27.50 + electricity
🗺️From Liepnitzsee home to Falkenberg: ~60 min southeast via A10 and B1. Total trip extension vs. going straight home: about 45 min extra.

Straussee

Strausberg, Brandenburg · ~45 min east · directly on route home
→ On the way home — zero detour

The practical choice — and genuinely good. Literally on your route home east from Spandau via the B1/B5. Straussee is 136 hectares with remarkably clear water (7m visibility), a small historic bathing marina, and the rare bonus of a dedicated dog swimming area. The town of Strausberg sits right on the lake, so there's a proper tarmac car park at the Kulturpark/Strandbad, a lakeside promenade to walk, and somewhere for coffee before the final 15 minutes home.

Parking & Access
📍Strandbad / Kulturpark car park, Strausberg — town tarmac car park at the lake bathing area. Walk to the shore in under 2 minutes.
52.5757°N, 13.8958°E
🛣️Drive straight in off the B1/B5 east through Strausberg — all tarmac, normal town roads, no issues for a large van.
🐕Dedicated dog swimming zone at the Strandbad — one of the few lakes near Berlin with a specific dog area. Orla will approve.
💶Strandbad entry ~€3–5pp · parking likely free or small fee
🏡From Straussee to home: ~15–20 minutes. Have a swim, eat a picnic, walk the shore, and you're home by lunchtime.
